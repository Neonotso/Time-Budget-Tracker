## Next Steps - Simple System to Start

### 1. Weekly Review (Sunday evenings)
- Review upcoming week
- Identify 3 Most Important Tasks (MITs) for the week
- Schedule time blocks for recurring tasks
- Capture any new tasks/projects

### 2. Daily Planning (Each morning)
- Identify 3 MITs for the day
- Time block for focused work
- Include buffer time for overflow/interruptions

### 3. Project Tracking
For each project, define:
- **Done criteria** (what does finished look like?)
- **Next action** (what's the very next physical step?)
- **Time estimate** (rough guess)
- **Due date/Target** (if applicable)

### 4. Energy-Based Scheduling
Match tasks to your energy levels:
- **High energy/focus**: Creative work, complex projects
- **Medium energy**: Admin tasks, learning, outreach prep
- **Low energy**: Physical chores, errands, administrative
